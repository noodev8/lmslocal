From a user perspective, we have several scenarios/hats

1 - The knowing pub owner (Paying our subscription)
They already have a pub and are manually running a LMS game through spreadsheets. They increase their funds with entry fees for charity and prizes while increasing customer satisfaction

2 - The un-knowing pub owner (May pay our subscription)
They already have a pub and have not heard or used LMS

3 - The admin
They want to organise a game for their local pub, social group or whoever

4 - The player
They enjoy the game, are in touch with either an admin, pub or both and want to join in. There may also be a price involved.


We need to link them together. The admin ultimately gets us through the door. This can also be a player.

The website must have a landing page but for different purposes and for different people and a login which directs them to heir home page

From there, depending on who they are, and their purpose must FLOW through the system with minimal friction

And each person can switch from one "type" to another easily only if they wanted to. ie. Easy to find the option but not get n the way of those who dont
